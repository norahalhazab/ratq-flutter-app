## 1. üö® THE PROBLEM

### The Healthcare Gap in Bangladesh: A Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Bangladesh faces a critical shortage of healthcare resources, with only **0.7 physicians per 1,000 people** (World Bank, 2021) and **59% of the population living in rural areas**. Aidx addresses these specific pain points directly:

#### 1. The Triage Bottleneck (Solved by: AI Symptom Analyzer)
*   **Problem:** With public hospitals overcrowded and specialists concentrated in cities, rural patients often wait days for basic consultations.
*   **Context:** Patients struggle to decide if a symptom is minor or an emergency, leading to either delayed care or unnecessary hospital visits.

#### 2. The "Medical Jargon" Barrier (Solved by: Report Scanner)
*   **Problem:** Lab reports are written in complex medical English, which is unintelligible to the average Bangladeshi patient.
*   **Context:** Patients often leave diagnostic centers with expensive test results but no understanding of what they mean until their next doctor's appointment.

#### 3. Emergency Response Latency (Solved by: SOS System)
*   **Problem:** In critical moments (accidents, heart attacks), finding an ambulance or notifying family is chaotic and uncoordinated.
*   **Context:** Bangladesh lacks a unified, rapid-response 911-style medical dispatch system that automatically shares location and medical ID.

#### 4. Medication Mismanagement (Solved by: Drug Info & Reminders)
*   **Problem:** Medication non-adherence and errors are common due to unclear instructions or forgetfulness.
*   **Context:** Patients often don't know the side effects of what they are taking, or they simply forget doses, leading to worsening chronic conditions.

#### 5. The Literacy & Tech Gap (Solved by: AI Voice Chat)
*   **Problem:** A significant portion of the population has low reading literacy or struggles with complex smartphone interfaces.
*   **Context:** Typing symptoms is a barrier for the elderly and rural users. They need to *speak* to a doctor, not type to a bot.

#### 6. Fragmented Discovery (Solved by: Hospital & Doctor Finder)
*   **Problem:** Finding the *right* specialist or the nearest open pharmacy is a word-of-mouth process.
*   **Context:** Patients waste critical time traveling to facilities that may be closed or lack the specific equipment/specialist they need.

#### 7. The "Golden Hour" Knowledge Gap (Solved by: First Aid Guide)
*   **Problem:** Bystanders often lack the knowledge to perform CPR or manage bleeding before an ambulance arrives.
*   **Context:** Preventable deaths occur because immediate first aid was not administered during the critical "golden hour."

#### 8. Disconnected Health Records (Solved by: Medical Timeline)
*   **Problem:** Medical history is stored in physical paper files that get lost, damaged, or forgotten at home.
*   **Context:** Doctors often treat patients without seeing their full history, leading to redundant tests and potential drug interactions.

#### 9. The "Silent Killer" (Solved by: Live Vitals Monitoring)
*   **Problem:** Chronic diseases like Hypertension and Diabetes are often unmonitored between doctor visits.
*   **Context:** Without continuous tracking, spikes in blood pressure or heart rate go unnoticed until a major health event occurs.

### **6.1 Phone Number Formatting**

**Purpose**: Convert user-entered phone numbers to Applink-compatible format

**Format Requirements**:

- Must include `tel:` prefix
- Example: `8801975355595` ‚Üí `tel:8801975355595`

**Implementation**:
```dart
static String formatPhoneNumber(String phoneNumber) {
  // Remove any existing tel: prefix and + sign
  String cleanNumber = phoneNumber.replaceAll('tel:', '').replaceAll('+', '');
  
  // Ensure it starts with 880
  if (cleanNumber.startsWith('01')) {
    cleanNumber = '88$cleanNumber'; // 01711111111 ‚Üí 88017111111111
  }
  
  // Add tel: prefix
  return 'tel:$cleanNumber';
}
```

**Examples**:
- `+8801975355595` ‚Üí `tel:8801975355595`
- `01711234567` ‚Üí `tel:8801711234567`
- `8801416177301` ‚Üí `tel:8801416177301`
